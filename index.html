Project Overview

After being tasked with redesigning a not-for-profit website of our choice - we chose a charity that one of our team members felt he owed something to for seeing his family through twenty years earlier. A support network who offers fun activities and camps for, not only the child diagnosed with cancer, but their siblings and parents who also need someone.

What's Wrong?

We scratched the surface of the ‘Challenge’ website and were immediately taken in by their bright colours and fun typography but after some digging we unearthed a whole toybox of trouble.

How can we help?

With such a worthy cause we wanted to help the ‘Challenge’ family: Increase their donations (what charity doesn’t) Communicate their purpose and mission clearly Offer the opportunity for everyone who wants to help Make it all about the kids! Show who we are actually doing it for



One hot, rainy night in January we gathered over our laptops to decide who might need our help? After much deliberation and some talk of sketchy logo’s and year’s gone by of forgotten 2007 sites we decided that a “Challenge” would be our challenge. The kids needed our help - the adults clearly didn’t have the time. We wanted to break down the key aspects of what was really needed to make this website great. The website is one of the most important tools a charity has to communicate and engage it’s viewers/users/donators - we wanted them all! And it was with this in mind… we started to think like a kid. What do you want from a charity? We constructed 8 quality questions each: When visiting a charity or Non for Profit website would you prefer to be taken on a journey or quickly & easily learn about the organization? What is it specifically that you donate - time, money, service, both? How often would you use a provided resource online to stay organised?
